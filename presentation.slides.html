by Marvellous Onuma-Kalu

Overview ¶

Risky borrowers are inevitable in the lending industry. Therefore while investors are willing to accept some degree of risk in their reach for larger return, the question is how much risk are they willing to incur?

Prosper, the first peer-to-peer lending platform in the United State had suffered from credit risk issues prior to reconstructing and launching a new credit system in July 2009. Although with the new credit system, Prosper has thus far seen improvement in borrower's loan performance, however some borrowers were unable to repay their loan for some reason.

From the Listing creation date to loan origination date, a borrower may qualify for a loan and has the capability to repay, but as time goes by, certain life event such as lost of job may cause such borrower to derail repayment of loans. This example is peculiar to borrowers who have obtained large loan original amount and thus has a longer loan term to pay-off.

This project covers an explanatory data analysis of Prosper Loan data. My goal is to investigate the loan performance of Prosper borrower's to determine if a borrower would default or not.

Data Structure ¶

I will use Prosper loan data for my analysis. The data which was downloaded from Udacity's course webpage, covers over 113, 937 loans funded between November 2005 and March 2014 on Prosper peer-to-peer lending platform. The dataset has 81 variables out of which I have selected 24 that are useful for my research question.

Based on our research goal, our key (dependent) variable for exploration is LoanStatus while variables such as borrower's financial information, credit history and debt information will be used as our independent variables to predict loan performance of borrowers.

Data Wrangling ¶

The Loan data was pretty clean, however for the purpose of my research goal and objective, the following data wrangling process were performed;

  • Loan Status included defaulted, completed, delinquent, charge-offs and current. I filtered the data to exclude current loan status as they are not useful for my analysis.

  • Some listings had unverified income, thus they were removed from the data.

  • I split the data into two subparts to include all listings before & since July 2009.

  • Finally general data cleaning such as removing outliers, data type conversion and dealing with missing values.

Let us now dive into our investigation by first looking at loan performance of Prosper's borrowers.

How has borrower's loan performance changed before & since July 2009?

Prosper launched a new credit system in July 2009 which is meant to improve their borrower's loan performance thereby decreasing default. Therefore investigating borrower's performance beforeand prior to this new credit system launch is highly important.

Using the ListingCreationDate variable in the data, I was able to split the dataset into two pieces, one containing all listings before July 2009 and the other containing all listings since July 2009. Statistically, there are 22522 listings created since July 2009 and 17484 listings created before July 2009.

  • Prior to July 2009, 64.87% of loans were good while 35.13% of loans were bad.
  • Since July 2009 when the new law was introduced, the percentage of good loans increased to 70.54% while that of bad loans decreased to 29.46%.

Why do some borrowers default their loan? ¶

Let us summarize some of the results we haveobtained from the dataset. The following are the reasons why loan borrowers default their loan

1) Interest rate: The higher the interest rate, the more likely will a borrower default a loan.

2) Borrowing beyond your capabilities: Certain type of loan seem to perform better than others. For example in the dataset, Green loans are more likely to defualt than any other loan. Moreso Borrowers who look at their neighboursand decide to collect loans to make purchases beyond what they can afford are more likely to default their loan.

3) Financial information: Borrower's with stable income and employment status are less likely to default a loan. In our dataset,

  • 75% of borrowers earn 6,250 (USD) monthly making it an annual income of 75,000 (USD), a reasonable amount for a living.
  • 75% of borrowers requested for a loan original amount of 9,000 (USD) to be paid off within 3 years at an interest rate of 26.94% (without loan charges).
  • This implies 75% of borrowers pay about 313 (USD) monthly for the loan they collect making it a total loan payment of 3,756 (USD) annually which is on the high side for just a loan payment.
  • 75% of borrowers have a debt-to-income ratio of 31%
  • There are more borrowers who are not home owners.

4) Credit History: Borrowers with clean credit history such as zero current delinquencies and zero delinquenciespast 7 years are less likely to default their loan. More than 75% of borrowers in do have a good credit history.

5) Occupation: Certain occupations also perform better with their loans than others. For example, we observe that Students (College Graduates) are the least risky borrowers in Prosper while Homemakers are the riskiest.

6) Borrower's Goegraphical location: Borrower's who live in expensive cities are more likely to default their loan.

7) Credit Score: The higher your credit score, the lower the interest rate you are likely to get from Prosper thus the less likley is your default rate.

D) Loan performance for different listing categories¶

The top five reasons why borrower's at Prosper apply for their loans are

1) Debt Consolidation, 40.39%

2) Reason not available, 19.07%

3) Other, 12.35%

4) Business, 7.10%

5) Home Improvement, 7.03%.

Thus most borrowers collect loans from Prosper to pay off debt faster and probably at a lower interest rate since they have good credit history as we have shown above. Let us look at the performance of these various loans.

From the barchat below,

  • We can observe that certain loans are doing better than others

  • Loans for Debt consolidation loans default 30.39% of the time.

  • Among all the loans, Motorcycle loans are the least risky defaulting only 11.63% of the time while Green loans are the riskiest defaulting 58.82% of the time. Other frequent defaulters are loans for household expenses and for medical and dental work, while people seem to completely pay Engagement Ring, RV and Boat loans quite frequently.

Since we see that Loans for debt consolidation has the highest count (40.39%) of all listing categories, and also default 30.39%, it is interesting to extend our investigations to include information about these category of borrowers.

Further analysis on the Occupation of borrowers shows that on average, Pharmacist then Doctors collect the largest loan original amount for debt consolidation.

Conclusion¶

We have analyzed some pattern in Prosper loan data and investigated loan default likelyhood of most borrowers. Overall, loans belonging to certain categories of borrowers are likely to default or not. More interestingly is the reason for collecting loans, we saw borrowers collect loans for different purposes, the top reason being for debt consolidation. We saw that the length of loan for most borrowers is 3 years and these category have more default rate that the other loan terms in the data.
